# Phase 1: Empathy Map

The Empathy Map below outlines the emotional and practical state of patients managing complex health schedules, helping guide our user-interface and alert features.

---

## Empathy Map Grid

### 1. THINK & FEEL
- "Will I remember to take all my pills today?"
- "I feel anxious when the doctor uses terms I don't understand."
- "I want to be proactive about my health but tracking sheets is tedious."

### 2. HEAR
- Doctor saying: "You must monitor your heart rate and take Lisinopril daily."
- Family members asking: "Did you take your pills this morning?"
- Health alerts on news about the importance of medication consistency.

### 3. SEE
- Multiple pill bottles clustered on the table.
- Missed appointment cards in the drawer.
- Complicated clinical terms on laboratory reports.

### 4. SAY & DO
- Tells family: "I am feeling fine, no symptoms," even when neglecting vitals logs.
- Writes reminders on sticky notes or kitchen calendars.
- Researches drug side effects on Google, sometimes getting incorrect info.

---

## Obstacles vs. Success Metrics

### PAINS (Obstacles & Frustrations)
- **Obstacle**: Forgetting the exact timing of medication.
- **Frustration**: Missing cardiology appointments because they were not on her phone.
- **Anxiety**: Reading words like "ECG review" and worrying about heart issues.

### GAINS (Wants & Success)
- **Goal**: A clean, unified timeline feed showing what pill to take when.
- **Support**: Automated text reminders sent directly to her phone.
- **Clarity**: Plain-English medical summaries of her appointments and vitals.
