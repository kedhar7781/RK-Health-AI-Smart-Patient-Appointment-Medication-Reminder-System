# Phase 1: Define Problem Statements

This document defines the core healthcare delivery problems that the **RK Health** portal solves for patients and practitioners.

---

## 1. The Core Problems

### Problem 1: Medication Non-Adherence
Patients managing chronic diseases or complex regimens often miss their pill times due to forgetfulness, lack of active notifications, or unscheduled schedules. 
- *Impact*: Reduced treatment efficacy, disease progression, and unnecessary emergency visits.

### Problem 2: Medical Jargon Complexity
Clinical documents (visit notes, prescription instructions, diagnostic labels) use complex terminology (e.g., "ECG", "Cardiology reviews", "Lisinopril"). Patients are often left confused, leading to anxiety and incorrect drug usage.
- *Impact*: Patients fail to follow doctor recommendations because they do not understand them.

### Problem 3: Disconnected Appointment Calendars
Doctor appointments are rarely synced automatically to the patient's primary calendar (like Google Calendar), leading to missed visits.
- *Impact*: Delayed treatment and high appointment cancellation rates for doctors.

---

## 2. Target User Persona
- **Name**: Sarah Jenkins (Chronic Hypertension Patient)
- **Age**: 58 years
- **Behavior**: Takes multiple daily pills (Lisinopril, Atorvastatin). Has frequent cardiologist consultations.
- **Pain Points**: Forgets pill times, struggles to keep track of appointment cards, and is overwhelmed by technical medical summaries from her doctor.

---

## 3. Empathy Map Summary
- **Says**: "I'm not sure what this medicine does," or "When is my next checkup?"
- **Thinks**: "I wish my calendar automatically sent me reminders," and "I want to understand what the doctor meant without searching Google."
- **Does**: Keeps physical notes, logs BP in paper journals, and sometimes skips doses.
- **Feels**: Overwhelmed, anxious about heart health, and determined to stay healthy.
